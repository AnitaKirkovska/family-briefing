# Example Morning Briefing

This is what your Family Briefing looks like when it lands in your Telegram or email every morning.

---

**Family Briefing — Thursday, Jul 10**

**SCHEDULING CONFLICTS (1):**
- Emma's soccer practice (4:00 PM @ Lincoln Field, Family Calendar) overlaps Liam's piano lesson (4:00 PM @ Home, Personal Calendar) by 60 min

**TODAY'S SCHEDULE (4):**
- 8:30 AM — School drop-off (Shared Family)
- 12:00 PM — Pediatrician appointment - Liam (Personal)
- 4:00 PM — Emma's soccer practice @ Lincoln Field (Family Calendar)
- 6:30 PM — Family dinner @ Home (Shared Family)

**FROM SCHOOL (3):**
- [Pickup Change] Emma: Early dismissal Friday at 1:15 PM | Flagged: early dismissal
- [Permission Slip] Liam: Field trip permission form due Monday | due 7/15
- [Spirit Day] Emma: Crazy Hair Day this Friday

**UPCOMING DEADLINES (2):**
- Liam: Field trip permission form — due 7/15
- Emma: Book order payment — due 7/18

---

## Notes

- Conflicts are shown at the top because they need a decision before the day starts. In this example, the parent needs to figure out who handles soccer pickup vs. piano lesson.
- School items are sorted by urgency: pickup changes first, then deadlines, then permission slips, then spirit days and reminders.
- Kid names appear when the parent has set them during onboarding. Without kid names, items show as "Early dismissal Friday at 1:15 PM" instead of "Emma: Early dismissal Friday."
- The briefing is delivered at the parent's chosen time (default 7:00 AM) via Telegram or email.
